Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our solar solutions.

An On-Grid system is connected to the public utility grid. You use solar power when available, and draw from the grid when it's not. Excess power can be sent back to the grid for credit. An Off-Grid system is completely independent. It requires batteries to store solar energy for use during the night or cloudy days, making it ideal for remote locations without grid access.

Most high-quality solar panels are designed to last for 25 to 30 years or more. While their efficiency might slightly decrease over time, they will continue to produce significant amounts of electricity for decades.

Yes, solar panels still generate electricity on cloudy or rainy days, although their output will be reduced compared to clear, sunny days. In an on-grid system, you will automatically draw from the grid during these times, while off-grid systems will rely on battery reserves.

Standard direct solar water pumps only operate when the sun is shining. If you need to pump water at night, you can either pump water into a storage tank during the day, or we can install a system that includes battery storage for night-time operation.

Solar panels are extremely low maintenance because they have no moving parts. Generally, you just need to keep them relatively clean and free of debris or dust. A simple wash with a hose a few times a year is usually sufficient, though heavy monsoons often do the cleaning for you.

We offer comprehensive warranties for all our installations. Solar panels typically come with a 25-year performance warranty. Inverters usually carry a 5 to 10-year warranty, and batteries vary by type but generally range from 3 to 10 years. We also provide a 1-year workmanship warranty on our installation services.
